Uno Health Member Portal

Case study

Problem

Uno Health enrolls partners with Medicare Advantage providers to help them re-certify their Medicare members in all state and federal programs they might be eligible for - putting thousands of dollars in their pockets.

Uno wanted to create their first member-facing portal to help plan members upload supporting evidence for their applications to programs like SNAP, Medicaid, Lifeline, and more.

Uno hired me for a two-week sprint to help Uno Health design and test an MVP for their member portal.

My process in developing the member portal MVP

Discovery

During the first week, I conducted internal interviews with Uno team members to learn what needs the portal was meant to solve, the most significant pain points facing members, and the constraints we needed to work with. I also looked at the existing materials shared with members to help them enroll by paper mail.

Aapplication information architecture

Design

After gaining an understanding of the existing process and context of the product, I began by sketching wireframes and defining the information architecture of the application. I continued to revise them with input from PMs, engineers, and compliance officers from the company. I created high-fidelity mockups to map out the user flow and built several prototypes to test out different mental models.

Screenshots demonstrating the main navigation of the application

Lowering friction during sign-up

One focus area was making sure that members could easily sign up for the app itself. Through research, we found that members had trouble creating online accounts and remembering their passwords so I worked with engineers to develop a “magic-link” sign up flow.

Working with PHI

The web-app needed provide and collect sensitive information about member’s finances, health, and more. This was Protected Health Information so we had the extra constraint of confirming members’ identities before they could start using the product.

Providing a strong call to action

The homepage features action cards that serve as clear calls to action for members when they use the app. I wanted to ensure that the design had room to scale as the team at Uno added additional functionality – the team could add additional cards with new features over time.

Helping members upload documents

The focus of the MVP was to allow members upload documents via the web-app. These documents helped Uno apply for benefits on the members’ behalf. Since a single member needed to upload numerous documents, I designed a checklist interface to help them keep track of what they had and hadn’t uploaded.

Responsive design

While I took a mobile-first approach in my designs I also ensured that my designs were responsive and provided examples of what the portal would look like on a desktop browser.

Feedback

I tested my designs with remote research participants I recruited using User Interviews and helped other team members test out the sign-up and onboarding flows with existing Uno Health members. Based on that feedback, I made multiple iterations on the product before preparing it for handoff to the team.

Deliverables

At the end of the sprint, I handed over tested designs for the MVP, examples of how the product would translate on desktop, detailed interaction specs, instructions for edge cases, and ideas for future development.

Outcome

The MVP I designed has received positive feedback from stakeholders and members and the team at Uno Health is investing more effort into it’s development.
